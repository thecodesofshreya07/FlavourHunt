Sushi Recipes | How to make Sushis | Homemade Sushi Recipes

Sushi may seem like a fancy dish reserved for restaurants, but making it at home is a fun and rewarding experience. With its fresh ingredients, delicate rolls, and bold flavors, sushi is both an art and a treat. Whether you're a fan of veggie fillings or seafood, homemade sushi lets you create your perfect roll just the way you like it—simple, delicious, and beautifully presented.

To prepare sushi, you’ll need sushi rice (short-grain rice), rice vinegar, sugar, and salt for seasoning the rice. Nori sheets (dried seaweed) form the outer layer. For the filling, you can choose from sliced vegetables like cucumber, carrot, and avocado, or seafood options like crab sticks, cooked shrimp, or raw fish if you prefer authentic sushi. Soy sauce, pickled ginger, and wasabi are typically served on the side.

Start by rinsing the sushi rice thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear. Cook the rice according to the package instructions, then let it sit covered for a few minutes once done. Meanwhile, prepare the seasoning by heating rice vinegar with a bit of sugar and salt until dissolved. Gently fold this mixture into the cooked rice while it’s still warm, then let the rice cool to room temperature. While the rice is cooling, slice your vegetables or seafood into thin, long strips. Place a bamboo sushi mat on a clean surface and cover it with plastic wrap to prevent sticking. Lay a sheet of nori on the mat, shiny side down. Wet your hands with water to keep the rice from sticking, then spread a thin layer of rice evenly over the nori, leaving about an inch of space at the top edge. Arrange your chosen fillings horizontally across the rice, about one-third of the way up from the bottom. Be careful not to overfill. Using the mat, begin to roll the sushi tightly from the bottom, pressing gently as you go. Once you reach the top edge, dab a little water on the exposed nori to seal the roll. Use a sharp knife to slice the roll into bite-sized pieces, cleaning the blade with a damp cloth between cuts for clean edges. Repeat the process with your remaining ingredients. Serve your sushi with soy sauce for dipping, a dab of wasabi for heat, and pickled ginger to cleanse the palate. Making sushi at home is a great way to explore flavors and enjoy a healthy, hands-on meal that’s as satisfying to prepare as it is to eat.
