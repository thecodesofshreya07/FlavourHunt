Kimchi Recipes | How to make Kimchi | Homemade Kimchi Recipes

Bold, tangy, and packed with flavor, kimchi is a Korean staple that has become a global favorite for its unique taste and incredible health benefits. Made through a simple fermentation process, kimchi brings together spicy, sour, and umami notes in a way that transforms ordinary vegetables into something extraordinary. Whether enjoyed as a side, in soups, or even on rice, homemade kimchi adds a punch of flavor to every bite.

To make traditional napa cabbage kimchi, you’ll need napa cabbage, coarse sea salt, water, and Korean red pepper flakes known as gochugaru. The seasoning paste includes garlic, ginger, fish sauce or soy sauce, sugar, and either Korean radish or carrot for crunch. Green onions are also added for freshness. You can adjust the spice level to your preference, making it as mild or fiery as you like.Begin by cutting the napa cabbage lengthwise into quarters, then into bite-sized pieces. Place the cabbage in a large bowl and sprinkle it generously with salt, tossing to coat every piece. Pour enough water to just cover the cabbage and let it soak for 1.5 to 2 hours, tossing occasionally. This step draws out moisture and softens the cabbage. Once done, rinse the cabbage thoroughly under cold water to remove the excess salt and drain well.

While the cabbage is soaking, prepare the kimchi paste. In a blender or food processor, combine garlic, ginger, sugar, and fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegetarian version). Blend into a smooth paste, then mix in gochugaru and a bit of water until you have a thick, red paste. Adjust the chili quantity based on how spicy you want it. Julienne the radish or carrot and chop the green onions, then mix them into the paste. Once the cabbage has been drained, place it in a large mixing bowl and gently combine it with the kimchi paste using gloved hands. Make sure every piece is well coated. This is the most satisfying part—massaging the spicy paste into the softened cabbage. Pack the kimchi tightly into clean, airtight jars or containers, pressing down to remove air pockets. Leave a bit of space at the top to allow for expansion as it ferments. Let the jars sit at room temperature for about 1–2 days, depending on your climate, then transfer them to the fridge. The flavor will deepen over time, becoming more sour and complex with each passing day. Kimchi can be enjoyed right away or left to ferment for a richer taste. It’s a vibrant, probiotic-rich food that adds a zesty kick to any dish and gives you a delicious way to explore traditional Korean flavors at home.
